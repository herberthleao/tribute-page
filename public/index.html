Chronology

Timeline of Paulo Freire's life:

  • 1921 — Paulo Reglus Neves Freire was born on September 19, in the city of Recife, Pernambuco.
  • 1927 — At the age of 6 he is literate by his parents.
  • 1931 — The Freires move to Jaboatão dos Guararapes, 18 kilometers from Recife.
  • 1934 — Temístocles Freire dies, a loving and affectionate man, father of Paulo Freire.
  • 1937 — Paulo Freire joins Colégio Osvaldo Cruz, in Recife as a fellow.
  • 1941 — He wins his first job as a teacher: he was invited to teach Portuguese at Colégio Osvaldo Cruz.
  • 1943 — Starts the law course at the Faculty of Law of Recife.
  • 1944 — Marries primary teacher Elza Maia Costa Oliveira.
  • 1944 — Paulo Freire is discharged from military service because he is teaching.
  • 1947 to 1954 — Assumes the function of Director of the Education and Culture Sector of the Social Service of Industry (SESI).
  • 1952 to 1961 — Professor at the School of Fine Arts at the University of Recife.
  • 1954 to 1956 — Exercises the position of superintendent of SESI.
  • 1956 to 1964 — He played an important role in SESI staff.
  • 1958 — He presents “Adult Education and marginal populations: the problem of hogs”, at the II National Congress on Adult Education.
  • 1959 to 1960 — He defends the thesis “Education and Brazilian Actuality” and obtains the title of Doctor in Philosophy and History of Education, at the University of Recife.
  • 1961 — Under the current law, he became a Free Lecturer at the School of Fine Arts at the University of Recife. Takes the portfolio of Philosophy and History of Education at the Faculty of Philosophy, Sciences and Letters of the University of Recife.
  • 1962 — He created the Cultural Extension Service (SEC), at the University of Recife and was its director until 1964.
  • 1962 to 1963 — Develops the first adult literacy experiences in Recife, using the “Paulo Freire Method”.
  • 1963 — Extends the experience with his Method to Natal (RN), João Pessoa (PB) and Angicos (RN). In Angicos, literate 300 workers. This work had great national and international repercussions.
  • 1963 — Begins the project that would be called, in 1964, National Literacy Program, literating adults in Gama, Brasília, Federal District.
  • 1964 — He coordinates the “National Literacy Program”, under the João Goulart government.
  • 1964 — Military coup in Brazil: dictatorship is established. Paulo Freire is imprisoned (political prison) for 70 days.
  • 1964 — Paulo Freire went into exile in Bolivia for a short time. That same year he went into exile in Chile because of the military coup in Bolivia.
  • 1967 — It publishes, in Brazil, Education as a practice of freedom.
  • 1969 — He teaches Harvard University for ten months.
  • 1970 — Publishes, in the United States, Pedagogy of the Oppressed.
  • 1970 — Moves to Geneva to work as a consultant in the Education Department of the World Council of Churches.
  • 1971 — Paulo Freire and a group of Brazilians found the Cultural Action Institute (IDAC).
  • 1975 to 1979 — He works with the IDAC Team in education and literacy programs in Guinea-Bissau, Cape Verde, Angola and São Tomé and Príncipe, in Africa.
  • 1979 — Under pressure from "internal exiles", the military government was forced to grant Amnesty. Paulo then obtained his first passport and visited Brazil.
  • 1980 — After 16 years, Paulo Freire definitely returns from exile.
  • 1980 — Joins as a professor at the Pontifical Catholic University of São Paulo (PUC-SP).
  • 1980 to 1990 — Professor at the State University of Campinas (UNICAMP).
  • 1986 — Receives the “Education for Peace” award from UNESCO, in Paris, France.
  • 1986 — Elza Maia Costa Oliveira Freire, Paulo Freire's first wife, passes away.
  • 1988 — Marries university professor Ana Maria Araújo Hasche, then Freire.
  • 1989 — Assumes the Secretary of Education of the Municipality of São Paulo, under Luiza Erundina. From 1989 to 1991, he worked to “change the face of the school”, in popular education policy. Implemented the MOVA-SP (Adult Literacy Movement) project.
  • 1991 — Participates in the creation of the Paulo Freire Institute.
  • 1992 — Publishes Pedagogy of Hope: a reunion with the Pedagogy of the Oppressed.
  • 1996 — Publishes Pedagogy of Autonomy: knowledge necessary for educational practice.
  • 1997 — Paulo Freire passes away at the age of 75, on May 2, of acute myocardial infarction, in the city of São Paulo.

    Paulo Freire participated in forums and debates during his lifetime. He held thousands of lectures and conferences. Gave opinions on the most diverse subjects. He gave interviews to newspapers, magazines and television. He became involved in progressive social movements, among many other activities as a militant and as an intellectual. He received awards, titles and honors worldwide, among these 39 titles of Doctor Honoris Causa, of which 5 were given to his widow.

    From 2000, his widow Ana Maria Araújo Freire, as legal successor to the work of Paulo Freire, organized his unpublished texts, named them and published in the “Paulo Freire Series”, of which she is director: Pedagogy of Indignation, Pedagogy of Possible Dreams and Pedagogy of Tolerance.
